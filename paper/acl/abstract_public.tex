We study how open-weights transformers (Qwen3-1.7B/8B, Gemma-2-9B)
represent a \textbf{relational operation} applied to an entity ---
\emph{currency-of}, \emph{capital-of}, \emph{language-of},
\emph{demonym-of}, \emph{continent-of} applied to a country. We find
that the operation is carried by a \textbf{manipulable operator
direction} in the residual stream that is largely \textbf{separable from
the operand}. Adding \texttt{v(op\_B)\ -\ v(op\_A)} to a prompt
performing \texttt{op\_A} converts the model\textquotesingle s answer to
\texttt{op\_B} for all 20 ordered operator pairs (matched-norm random
controls null), and the direction \textbf{generalizes to operands never
used to build it}, to \textbf{unseen paraphrase frames} (every
build\ensuremath{\to}test frame combination flips), and \textbf{across architectures}
--- the full pipeline replicates on Gemma-2-9B with the largest effect
sizes of the three models tested. A two-way analysis of variance
factorizes the representation as
\texttt{H{[}operand,\ operator{]}\ =\ \ensuremath{\mu}\ +\ operand\ +\ operator\ +\ interaction},
\textasciitilde90\% additive with a \textasciitilde10\% \textbf{fusion}
interaction, operator and operand occupying largely orthogonal
subspaces; the operation-dominant representation \emph{emerges along the
sequence} (operand-dominant at the entity token \ensuremath{\to} operator-dominant at
the query token). Strikingly, the operation is \textbf{separable from
its surface realization}: \emph{language-of} and \emph{demonym-of} are
distinct operator directions even though both emit "Italian". As an
explanatory device we borrow a morphological metaphor --- the model
\textbf{declines} a concept by its functional role (case), with
\textbf{syncretism} (shared surface form) and \textbf{fusion}
(non-additive stem+ending) as in a Latin declension. Testing
generalization across domains, the factorization is \textbf{specific to
relational retrieval}: for \textbf{arithmetic} (+, \ensuremath{\times}, -) and
\textbf{comparison-logic} operators, the interaction term is 2--4\ensuremath{\times}
larger and the operator \textbf{fails to generalize to held-out
operands}, consistent with arithmetic being computed by a "bag of
heuristics" rather than a linear operator. A control that instead varies
a \emph{linear numeric parameter} (add-N) rather than the function
generalizes better and is near-collinear, reconciling a contrary recent
report (\citealp{christ2025structure}). Finally, the structure is \textbf{causal,
not a readout advantage}: the Jacobian-lens ("J-space") readout is not
more legible than the raw residual on any of our metrics under matched
controls.
