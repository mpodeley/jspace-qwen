\section{Introduction}

When a language model completes \emph{"The currency of Italy is---"},
does it represent \emph{currency-of-Italy} as one fused thing, or as an
entity plus an operation applied to it? This paper argues, causally, for
the second reading --- and asks the question a claim like that must
answer at \textbf{three separate levels of evidence}:
\textbf{representation} (does a stable relational direction
\emph{exist}, separable from the operand?), \textbf{causal influence}
(does intervening on it systematically move the model\textquotesingle s
\emph{relative preference} between answers?), and \textbf{behavioral
sufficiency} (can the factorized parts make the model actually
\emph{produce} the target answer?). The levels matter because
margin-moving interventions are routinely read as if they controlled
behavior. Here that gap becomes the object of study --- and resolves: it
is a property of \emph{how} the direction is applied (mode and dose),
not of \emph{what} it contains.

A line of work shows that a \emph{task} or \emph{relation} can be
captured by a single addable vector in an LLM\textquotesingle s residual
stream (task vectors, \citealp{hendel2023icl}; function vectors, \citealp{todd2024function}), that many relations are approximately linear operators (LRE,
\citealp{hernandez2024lre}; \citealp{merullo2024vector}), and that recall can be read
as function execution over components at different positions (\citealp{wang2025functional}). What has not been quantified is the \textbf{joint structure of
operator and operand within one prediction state}: whether the operation
\emph{factorizes} from its argument there, \emph{generalizes}, is
separable from the \emph{word it produces}, and suffices to \emph{drive
generation} --- and whether any of this holds beyond relational facts.
We answer these on Qwen3 and Gemma-2, across a geographic and an
animal-taxonomy domain, with arithmetic and logic as the contrast. A
closing discussion (\S{}5) offers \textbf{declension} as the organizing
metaphor for the asymmetries observed.

\section{Related work and what is new
here}

\textbf{Extracting relational concepts, and knowledge recall as function
execution.} The closest prior line is \citet{wang2024locating}, who locate
hidden states expressing a relation separately from its subject and
transplant them between subjects --- and its successor \textbf{\citet{wang2025functional}}, which abstracts recall into a functional structure (subject
activations as \emph{input argument}, relation activations as
\emph{function body}, object activations as \emph{return value}),
verified by patching and counter-knowledge swaps of each component. Our
object of study differs in four ways: they localize and exchange
components read at \emph{different token positions}, while we quantify
the \textbf{joint operator \ensuremath{\times} operand decomposition of a single
prediction state}; we show the steering vector \emph{is} that
decomposition\textquotesingle s operator main effect, numerically; we
validate against \textbf{competitive semantic nulls} (permuted labels,
within-subspace random); and we establish \textbf{generative sufficiency
by additive reconstruction}, including components built without the
target cell and an out-of-sample dose calibration. Their framing is
function \emph{execution across positions}; ours is state
\emph{composition at one position} --- complementary readings of the
same recall machinery.

\textbf{Operation as an addable vector; relation as a linear operator.}
Task Vectors (\citealp{hendel2023icl}) and Function Vectors (Todd et
al., ICLR 2024) extract one vector per ICL task and add/patch it
(similarly \citealp{liu2024icv}; \citealp{turner2023actadd}) --- but bundle
operator \emph{and} operand into one unfactored direction, without
competitive nulls. LRE (\citealp{hernandez2024lre}) fits a
\emph{monolithic} affine map per relation. Summing Up the Facts
(\citealp{chughtai2024summing}) decomposes recall into additive \emph{circuits};
ours is a two-way effects decomposition of the \textbf{residual state
itself}, with a measured interaction term and a sufficiency test on the
components.

\textbf{Attribute subspaces / reading position.} RAVEL (\citealp{huang2024ravel}) disentangles multiple \emph{attributes of one entity} into
subspaces (operators sharing an operand); we measure the orthogonal
axis, \textbf{operator vs operand}. The operand\ensuremath{\to}operator shift along the
sequence restates the subject-enrichment \ensuremath{\to} attribute-extraction dynamics
of \citet{geva2023dissecting} in operator/operand terms --- our least
novel point, included as confirmation.

\textbf{Global workspace and the Jacobian lens.} \citet{gurnee2026workspace} introduce the Jacobian lens and identify a
mid-depth "global workspace" band. We adopt their depth band as the site
of intervention; our contribution is orthogonal --- the operator/operand
structure of what the band \emph{holds} --- and we report a controlled
null on the lens\textquotesingle s readout advantage for our task
(\S{}4.9).

\textbf{Arithmetic and logic.} LLM arithmetic is reported as a "bag of
heuristics" (\citealp{nikankin2025heuristics}) and Fourier/helical procedures (Nanda
et al. 2023; \citealp{zhong2023clock}; \citealp{kantamneni2025trig}), with the
operand \emph{value} cleanly linear (\citealp{gurnee2024space}); truth is
steerable (\citealp{marks2023geometry}) and logical-operator heads exist (Hong
et al. 2024). No prior work reports a \textbf{cross-domain
operator/operand factorization}; we provide one and show where it does
not emerge.

\textbf{What is new here is the combination, not any single ingredient.}
That relations admit addable vectors and linear decoders, and that
recall components can be located and swapped across positions, is
established; what has not been done is the joint, quantified analysis at
one position: the operator \ensuremath{\oplus} operand \textbf{factorization of the
residual state itself}, with the steering vector shown to \emph{be} the
ANOVA operator component; the all-pairs swap validated against
\textbf{permuted-label and within-subspace nulls};
\textbf{held-out-operand generalization}; the \textbf{compositional
sufficiency} test (does a state assembled from the parts
\emph{generate}?) with its dose \ensuremath{\times} position calibration; the
\textbf{operation-vs-realization dissociation}; and the
\textbf{cross-domain test} (each claim, its evidence, and its decisive
control are tabulated at the head of \S{}4). The declension reading is our
\emph{presentation} of these measurements, not an additional empirical
claim.

\section{Method}

\textbf{Setup.} Qwen3-1.7B/8B throughout, plus Gemma-2-9B for the
cross-architecture replication (\S{}4.7); all run in bf16 on a single AMD
Strix Halo APU (environment details in the supplementary repository). A
relation is rendered into a template; the canonical frame is
\texttt{"The\ \{op\}\ of\ \{a\}\ is"}, and \S{}4.5 additionally uses two
paraphrase frames (question--answer and discourse-prefixed) that hold
the \texttt{\{op\}\ of\ \{a\}} unit fixed while varying the surrounding
frame, all ending in \emph{is} so answer scoring is comparable. Reading
position is the query token unless stated; the depth "workspace" band
(38--92\% of layers) is where \citet{gurnee2026workspace} locates the global
workspace and where we build operator directions.

\textbf{Notation.} The model\textquotesingle s state is a grid
\texttt{h\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},t\}\ \ensuremath{\in}\ R\^{}d}: one residual-stream vector per layer
\texttt{\ensuremath{\ell}} per token position \texttt{t}; every measurement fixes both
indices. Two reading positions recur: the \textbf{entity token} (the
operand\textquotesingle s last subword, \texttt{t\ =\ -2} in the
canonical frame) and the \textbf{query token} (the final prompt token,
\texttt{t\ =\ -1}, where the next-token prediction is read; "query"
means reading position, not attention\textquotesingle s Q). Rendering
the operator--operand grid through one template gives, at each
\texttt{(\ensuremath{\ell},\ t)}, a family of states \texttt{h\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},t\}(o,\ k)}.

\textbf{Operator direction \ensuremath{\equiv} factorization component.} For operator
\texttt{k},
\texttt{v\_\ensuremath{\ell}(k)\ =\ mean\_o{[}\ h\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},-1\}(o,\ k)\ -\ mean\_\{k\textquotesingle{}\}\ h\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},-1\}(o,\ k\textquotesingle{})\ {]}}
--- one direction \textbf{per layer} of the workspace band (13 layers
per model), the function-vector construction. This is
\emph{algebraically identical} to the operator main effect of the
two-way factorization below
(\texttt{b\_\ensuremath{\ell}(k)\ =\ mean\_o\ h\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},-1\}(\textperiodcentered{},k)\ -\ \ensuremath{\mu}\_\ensuremath{\ell}}), and we verify
the identity numerically (max relative deviation 8.5\ensuremath{\times}\ensuremath{10^{-8}} across the
band). Everything the paper does --- steering, composing, decoding ---
manipulates this one object; the differences between experiments are
differences in \emph{how it is applied}, never in what is extracted. We
characterize it as a \textbf{relation-conditioned retrieval operator}
--- it conditions retrieval on the queried relation; we make no claim
about the attention/MLP circuitry that implements the binding.

\textbf{Two-way effects decomposition.} At a fixed \texttt{(\ensuremath{\ell},\ t)}:
\texttt{h\ =\ \ensuremath{\mu}\ +\ a(o)\ +\ b(k)\ +\ e(o,\ k)} --- operand main effect,
operator main effect, and \textbf{interaction (cell residual)}, every
term carrying the \texttt{(\ensuremath{\ell},\ t)} indices. The identity is exact
because the design is balanced and \texttt{e} is defined as the
residual; this is a descriptive decomposition, not an inferential ANOVA,
and with one observation per cell \texttt{e} also absorbs any
cell-specific idiosyncrasy. Variance shares are
\texttt{SS\_a\ =\ K\textperiodcentered{}\ensuremath{\Sigma}\_o\ensuremath{\,\Vert\,}a(o)\ensuremath{\,\Vert\,}\ensuremath{^{2}},\ SS\_b\ =\ O\textperiodcentered{}\ensuremath{\Sigma}\_k\ensuremath{\,\Vert\,}b(k)\ensuremath{\,\Vert\,}\ensuremath{^{2}},\ SS\_e\ =\ \ensuremath{\Sigma}\_\{o,k\}\ensuremath{\,\Vert\,}e(o,k)\ensuremath{\,\Vert\,}\ensuremath{^{2}}},
each reported as its fraction of \texttt{SS\_a\ +\ SS\_b\ +\ SS\_e} (O
operands, K operators). Table 1 uses the mid-workspace layer ---
\textbf{layer 17 of 28 (63\% depth) at 1.7B, 23 of 36 at 8B, 27 of 42 on
Gemma-2-9B} --- at \texttt{t\ =\ -1} and, as the control,
\texttt{t\ =\ -2}.

\textbf{Swap, positions, and controls.} Efficacy of
\texttt{k\_A\ \ensuremath{\to}\ k\_B} is the change in
\texttt{logit(answer\_B)\ -\ logit(answer\_A)} at the query position
when \texttt{\ensuremath{\alpha}\textperiodcentered{}(v(k\_B)\ -\ v(k\_A))} is added --- over the band or a
single layer, at all prompt positions or a chosen one (all / query /
operand / sentence-initial control). Beyond the \textbf{matched-norm
random} direction, the null battery includes directions rebuilt under
\textbf{permuted relation labels} (per-operand and global --- same
statistics, no semantics: the decisive null), random directions
\textbf{inside the operator subspace}, the correct direction at the
\textbf{wrong layer} and \textbf{wrong position}, an
\textbf{other-relation} direction of matched norm, and
\textbf{shuffled-answer} scoring. Every intervention reports margin
change, normalized margin, target rank before/after, top-1, short-decode
containment (\S{}below), on-task KL, and off-task KL on unrelated WikiText
--- not flips alone. Answers are scored at their distinguishing first
token (bare digit for arithmetic); a tokenization guard drops, per
operator pair, operands whose answers collide there (4 of 12 survive on
the syncretic demonym\ensuremath{\leftrightarrow}language pairs; all 12 elsewhere).

\textbf{Composition (behavioral sufficiency).} The
factorization\textquotesingle s component vectors, computed per layer,
are recombined and \textbf{replace} the residual at the query position
(\texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}+operator}, \texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}+operand}, \texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}+operand+operator},
\texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}+interaction}, the full donor, a norm-matched magnitude
control) --- plus two loophole-closers: a \textbf{leave-one-cell-out}
reconstruction whose components never saw the target cell, and a
\textbf{wrong-operand} composition that should (and does) redirect the
answer. Three named generation readouts, used consistently and never
cross-compared: \textbf{short-decode containment} (3-token greedy
continuation contains the gold answer, case-insensitive --- \emph{the
euro}, \emph{euros}, \emph{Euro} all count; the model\textquotesingle s
clean accuracy under the same readout is the ceiling);
\textbf{long-decode classification} (k = 8, each raw generation labeled
target / source / wrong-entity / wrong-relation / degraded); and
\textbf{forced-choice sequence probability} (length-normalized
log-probability of each gold answer under the same intervention ---
immune to article interception and multi-token variants).

\textbf{Generalization and domains.} Build \texttt{v(op)} from half the
operands; test the swap on the held-out half --- a genuine operator vs.
interpolation among build examples. Domains: five country relations
(currency, capital, language, demonym, continent \ensuremath{\times} 12 countries), an
independently curated \textbf{animal-taxonomy domain} (class, habitat,
diet, covering \ensuremath{\times} 12 animals; stable 1-to-1 answers, tokenizer-screened,
three paraphrase frames), and arithmetic / comparison logic as the
contrast domains.

\textbf{Statistical treatment.} The ordered swaps are \textbf{not}
independent observations: they share operator directions and operands.
We report cluster-bootstrap percentile intervals (10,000 replicates) at
two levels: within a pair the \textbf{operand} is the resampling unit;
across the paradigm the \textbf{operator} is the top-level cluster (a
dyadic node bootstrap --- resample operators with replacement, weight
each pair by its endpoints\textquotesingle{} multiplicities, resample
operands within surviving pairs). Because only five operator clusters
exist, we additionally report \textbf{leave-one-operator-out}: dropping
any operator leaves the contrast in {[}+18.8, +25.8{]} at 1.7B and
{[}+23.3, +28.9{]} at 8B with flip fraction 1.00 throughout --- no
single operator carries the paradigm. Composition claims (\S{}4.2) use
paired per-cell differences under the same hierarchical bootstrap.
Per-operand values are released as long-form artifacts (Appendix A).

\textbf{J-space controls.} We repeat readout-geometry in the J-lens
readout \texttt{unembed(J\textperiodcentered{}h)} vs the logit-lens readout
\texttt{unembed(h)}, and re-run efficacy with \textbf{spectrum-matched
random-projection} and \textbf{permuted-vocabulary} null lenses
(Appendix B).

\subsection{Relational operators are causally manipulable --- and
the effect is localized and
label-specific}

All 20 ordered operator swaps flip the \textbf{target-vs-source logit
margin} while the matched-norm random control is \ensuremath{\approx} 0. Under the
operator-level cluster bootstrap (\S{}3), the swap-random contrast is
\textbf{+22.6, 95\% CI {[}+14.0, +32.1{]}} at 1.7B, \textbf{+26.0
{[}+17.9, +32.8{]}} at 8B, and \textbf{+30.4 {[}+23.8, +34.8{]}} on
Gemma-2-9B; the flip fraction is \textbf{1.00 {[}1.00, 1.00{]}} in all
three. Per-pair operand-bootstrap CIs never cross zero, and the weakest
pairs are exactly the syncretic ones (demonym \ensuremath{\leftrightarrow} language, which share
their surface form; per-pair distributions in Appendix A).

\textbf{Where the vector has to land (1.7B, \ensuremath{\alpha} = 4).} Injecting at the
\textbf{query token alone} reproduces the all-position effect on the
margin (+28.7 {[}+16.1, +39.8{]} vs. +28.9 {[}+16.2, +39.9{]}) at
\textbf{60\ensuremath{\times} lower off-task cost} (KL 0.29 vs. 18.4 nats/token), and one
late layer at the query token brings the target to median rank 69 at
near-zero cost (0.04); the operand token and a structurally irrelevant
token do essentially nothing (+1.8 / +2.3). Full position \ensuremath{\times} scope
battery in Appendix A.

The margin effect is a \textbf{localized edit of the queried relation}
--- position-specific (sign-correct 98/98/91\% for the effective
conditions vs. 4--16\% for the controls) --- not a global perturbation;
it replicates at 8B (query-only +32.7 vs. all +33.2, at 63\ensuremath{\times} lower
off-task cost).

\textbf{The label is everything.} Directions rebuilt from the
\emph{same} residuals with \textbf{permuted relation labels} --- same
statistics, no semantics --- abolish the effect: \textbf{+0.7 {[}-0.8,
+3.9{]}} per-operand, +0.6 global (20 redraws; 8B +0.15 / +0.09 vs. real
+26.0). A random direction \textbf{inside the operator subspace} also
does nothing: +0.75 {[}-2.2, +3.5{]} (8B +0.43). Three probes stay
nonzero for \emph{mechanistically informative} reasons and decompose the
margin: an \textbf{other-relation} direction keeps the shared
\texttt{-v(source)} and yields \ensuremath{\approx} half the effect (+11.1 --- uninstalling
the source is half the margin); \textbf{shuffled-answer scoring} still
moves (+21.1) because \texttt{+v(target)} boosts the whole answer
\emph{category} --- operand specificity lives in generation, which \S{}4.2
measures; and the \textbf{wrong (early) layer} retains +9.8 because
residual additions \emph{persist} downstream (locality measured properly
in \S{}4.6; forest plot in Appendix A).

\textbf{Dose--response, both signs.} The effect is a \textbf{signed
causal axis}: the operator-specific shift is monotone in \ensuremath{\alpha} through zero
and \emph{reverses} with the direction --- +22.6 at \ensuremath{\alpha} = +4 vs.
\textbf{-21.0 at \ensuremath{\alpha} = -4}, an almost exact mirror (8B: +26.0 vs. -24.5),
saturating at both ends (the positive branch higher, +29 vs. -15 raw,
because the clean margin already favors the source: a floor) --- while
the random control\textquotesingle s shift (\ensuremath{\approx} +6) is flat in
\textbar \ensuremath{\alpha}\textbar, which is why every headline number is a swap -
random contrast. Off-task cost grows with dose and breadth (7.9 \ensuremath{\to} 21
nats/token band-wide vs. 0.29 at the query token). What dose does to
\emph{generation} is the subject of \S{}4.2. (Curves in Appendix A.)

\subsection{A composed residual trajectory generates the
answer}

At the default dose, additive steering flips every \emph{margin} but
rarely makes the model \emph{say} the target answer (0.4--2.2\%
short-decode containment), whereas replacing the query-position residual
with a \textbf{real donor activation} (same operand, target relation)
does --- 51\%, at the model\textquotesingle s clean accuracy of 53\%. Is
the donor carrying something the direction lacks --- operand binding, a
non-additive interaction, lexical or execution signals? We decompose the
donor by the exact factorization and patch partial reconstructions back
in. To be precise about the object: what is patched is a
\textbf{per-layer factorized residual trajectory} --- one reconstructed
vector per workspace-band layer, all at the query position --- not a
single-layer vector (the single-layer variant is reported at the end of
this section; every variant includes \ensuremath{\mu}, since the hook \emph{replaces}
the state and a replacement must be a plausible full-magnitude
residual). 1.7B, 224 (pair, operand) cells:

\textbf{The donor carries nothing detectable that the additive parts
don\textquotesingle t.} The paired per-cell difference composed - donor
is \textbf{+0.9 pp, 95\% CI {[}-6.9, +8.3{]}} at 1.7B (51.8\% vs.
50.9\%; clean ceiling 53\%) and -3.1 pp {[}-12.5, +5.2{]} at 8B (62.1\%
vs. 65.2\%; ceiling 68\%); adding the interaction term changes nothing
detectable (-0.9 pp {[}-8.3, +6.9{]}; interaction \emph{alone}: 7.1\%,
near the 4.5\% magnitude control). Honest limit: with five operator
clusters a formal \ensuremath{\pm}5 pp equivalence test is underpowered --- the claim
is "no detectable gap," not certified equivalence. Two controls close
the loopholes. A \textbf{leave-one-cell-out} reconstruction, built
\emph{without ever seeing the target cell}, still generates at 35.7\%
(8B: 50.4\%) --- composition, not leakage --- though genuinely below the
donor (paired -15 pp, CI excluding zero at 8B). And a \textbf{wrong
operand\textquotesingle s} component redirects the answer to \emph{that}
operand (34.4\% says-other vs. 1.8\% baseline; 8B: 48.2\%): the operator
selects the relation, the operand selects whose answer.

\textbf{Sufficiency by steering, at the calibrated dose.} Since
\texttt{v(op)} \emph{is} the operator component (\S{}3), adding
\texttt{\ensuremath{\alpha}\textperiodcentered{}(v(B)\ -\ v(A))} at the query position of one layer lands, at
\ensuremath{\alpha} = 1, on \texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}\ +\ operand\ +\ operator(B)\ +\ interaction(A)} ---
the composed state up to a wrong interaction term. The dose sweep
confirms it behaviorally: generation follows an \textbf{inverted-U
peaked exactly at the on-manifold dose} --- 38.4\% at \ensuremath{\alpha} = 1 for a single
layer, \textbf{51.3\% at \ensuremath{\alpha} \ensuremath{\approx} 0.1 for the 13-layer band} (band additions
accumulate, so the band\textquotesingle s on-manifold dose is \ensuremath{\approx} 1/13 per
layer; the same peak doses reproduce at 8B: 45.1\% and 56.2\%) --- while
past the peak the margin keeps climbing (+28.9 at \ensuremath{\alpha} = 4) as generation
collapses. The dose is not tuned post hoc: \textbf{calibrating \ensuremath{\alpha} on half
the operands and evaluating frozen on the disjoint half}, \ensuremath{\alpha}* is
identical in all five partitions (0.1 band, 1.0 single layer), held-out
generation at frozen \ensuremath{\alpha}* is 60.8\% {[}56.2, 67.3{]} (single layer
42.2\%), and the evaluation-optimal dose coincides with the calibrated
one in 10/10 splits. The earlier "steering moves margins but cannot
generate" result was an \textbf{overdose artifact}, not an information
deficit: the factorized representation is \textbf{compositionally
sufficient}, by replacement and by calibrated addition. (Single layer:
\texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}\ +\ operator} alone generates at 44.6\% --- unpatched layers
still carry the operand.)

\textbf{The generation audit} (Appendix A) closes the metric loopholes
directly. Classifying every raw k = 8 generation: the calibrated dose
produces the \emph{fluent, correct} answer (59.4\% target vs. 0.9\%
source; composed patch 57.6\% --- both at the clean
prompt\textquotesingle s own 58.5\%), while at the overdose
\textbf{94.6\% of generations are degraded token loops} by manual
inspection --- category-correct, fluency-destroyed, not surface variants
the metric missed. A \textbf{forced-choice} readout (full-sequence
log-prob among the operand\textquotesingle s gold answers, under the
intervention) then dissociates information from fluency: the target wins
at \textbf{80.4\% even at the overdose} (87.5\% calibrated, 84.4\% donor
patch, 2.7\% clean). Overdosing destroys the generation manifold, not
the encoded preference.

\textasciitilde90\% additive; operand and operator components occupy
distinct subspaces (principal angles 41--85\textdegree{} at the query token ---
near-orthogonality is the high-dimensional default, so the load-bearing
evidence is the variance split and the causal results). Since the
steering vector is numerically this decomposition\textquotesingle s
operator main effect (\S{}3), the direction that flips margins (\S{}4.1), the
component that composes into a generating state (\S{}4.2), and the term
that dominates this split are \textbf{one vector seen three ways}. The
entity enters operand-dominant and is re-marked by its queried role at
the query position (the reading-position control rules out template
echo).

(A PCA of the 60 workspace states, showing the operand-organized cloud
at the entity token reorganizing into operator clusters at the query
token, is in Appendix A.)

\subsection{Operation \ensuremath{\neq} realization
(syncretism)}

\emph{language-of} and \emph{demonym-of} emit the \textbf{same word}
(Italian) yet are \textbf{distinct operator directions}; the swap
between them is weak precisely because they share their output word. An
\textbf{exponent-free operator marker} --- "exponent" in the
morphological sense: the surface form that realizes a feature, here the
shared answer token --- built from the shared-output pairs
(\texttt{mean{[}h(language)\ -\ h(demonym){]}}, the output word
cancelled by construction) still installs the relation causally (1.7B:
-3.6 \ensuremath{\to} +12.5): the operator representation is not an output-word
representation (\S{}5 reads this as syncretism).

(Operator-direction cosines and the exponent-free-marker bar chart are
in Appendix A.)

\subsection{Operators generalize: held-out operands, prompt frames,
wordings --- and a second
domain}

\textbf{Held-out operands.} \texttt{v(op)} built on 6 operands and
applied to the 6 held-out still flips \textbf{20/20} swaps --- a
transferable operator, not interpolation: contrast \textbf{+20.0
{[}+10.8, +29.5{]}} at 1.7B, \textbf{+22.8 {[}+14.0, +31.0{]}} at 8B,
flip fraction 1.00 at both. This is not one lucky split: over five
shuffled partitions every pair flips in every partition (contrast range
{[}+20.0, +22.9{]}; 8B {[}+22.8, +25.6{]}), and full
leave-one-country-out gives 220/224 flips at 1.7B and 222/224 at 8B.

\textbf{Frames and wordings.} Across three paraphrase frames, every
build\ensuremath{\to}test combination flips --- 1.7B \textbf{180/180},
\emph{frame-invariant to two decimals} (+22.6 on all test frames); 8B
100/100. Stronger: the direction survives re-lexicalizing the
\emph{relation itself} (\emph{currency of} \ensuremath{\to} \emph{money used in},
\ldots) both ways, \textbf{40/40 at both scales}, transfer \ensuremath{\approx} within
(+22.59 vs. +22.62). Clean baselines shift; the causal effect does not:
it is a property of the \textbf{relation}, not its wording.

\textbf{A second, non-geographic domain.} Every signature replicates on
an independently curated animal-taxonomy domain (class / habitat / diet
/ covering \ensuremath{\times} 12 animals; 1.7B): \textbf{12/12} ordered swaps flip (+18.1
{[}+13.5, +22.8{]}); held-out operands \textbf{12/12} at +18.0 ---
transfer indistinguishable from within; cross-frame \textbf{72/72},
again frame-invariant to two decimals; permuted-label and subspace nulls
at zero (-0.5 and +0.2, CIs spanning 0); the same reading-position
signature (operand 79\% at the entity token, operator 62\% at the query
token); and even the same quantitative margin decomposition (the
other-relation probe retains \ensuremath{\approx} half the effect in both domains: 47\% vs.
49\%). Two honest caveats: the animal factorization carries more
interaction (22\% vs. 9\% --- informative in \S{}4.8), and the
generation-level readout is floor-limited here (the
model\textquotesingle s own clean greedy accuracy on these multi-token
answers is 6\%), so in this domain sufficiency is attested by margins
and rank, not exact match.

\subsection{Where the operator is represented is not where it
acts}

A per-layer sweep (1.7B; direction built \textbf{and} injected at each
single layer) separates three notions the band framing conflates.
\emph{Trivial readability:} a held-out-operand classifier decodes the
operator at \textbf{100\% from every layer including layer 0} --- the
operator\textquotesingle s token is in the prompt; decodability locates
nothing. \emph{Representation:} the operator share peaks \textbf{early}
(92\% at 19\% depth), decaying to 57\%. \emph{Causal leverage:} \ensuremath{\approx} 0
early, peaking \textbf{late} (+21.3 at 89\% depth \ensuremath{\approx} the full
band\textquotesingle s +22.6). The informative curves peak \textbf{70
depth-points apart} (8B: 23\% vs. 89\%): where the operator is most
cleanly factorized is not where injecting it most moves the output ---
being \emph{readable}, \emph{the representation}, and \emph{causally
potent} are three different layer profiles of one direction (explains
the wrong-layer probe of \S{}4.1; figure in Appendix A).

\subsection{Cross-architecture replication
(Gemma-2-9B)}

The entire pipeline transfers unchanged to \textbf{Gemma-2-9B} ---
different corpus, tokenizer, and architecture family. Every qualitative
signature replicates, with the strongest effects of the three models:
all-pairs \textbf{+30.4 {[}+23.8, +34.8{]}} (20/20), held-out
\textbf{+26.6 {[}+20.6, +33.2{]}}, operator variance 84.8\% with 6.8\%
interaction (full table in Appendix A). Raw logit units are not
comparable across architectures (Gemma-2 soft-caps logits) --- compare
flip rates and variance shares, which match or exceed
Qwen3\textquotesingle s: the factorization is not an idiosyncrasy of one
family.

\textbf{Reconciling a contrary report.} \citet{christ2025structure} find that
\textbf{add-N} operators (a fixed addend \texttt{N} over one number)

\emph{parameter} --- effectively an operand on the number line (Gurnee
\& Tegmark 2024) --- while our \texttt{+\ \ensuremath{\times}\ -} cut varies the
\emph{function}. Running both cuts on Qwen3 places add-N between
relations and \texttt{+\ \ensuremath{\times}\ -} (held-out 5/12 \textperiodcentered{} 6/12), and its
directions are by far the most collinear of the three families (76\% of

operations). Their positive and our negative coexist without
contradiction; the full table is in Appendix A.

\section{Discussion}

\textbf{Modes of intervention, and what a margin measures.} What looked
like a gap between influencing a preference and executing an operation
was a gap between \emph{ways of applying the same vector}: a large added
multiple moves margins while pushing the state off-manifold; a
calibrated multiple, or replacement with the composed state, generates
at ceiling (\S{}4.2). The null battery likewise decomposes the margin ---
half uninstalling the source, half installing the target
\emph{category}, operand specificity visible only in generation (\S{}4.1).
Both points travel: margin shifts at one fixed dose measure
category-level preference under a possibly off-manifold intervention,
and negative generation results do not, by themselves, show the
information is absent. The three-level discipline --- representation,
influence, sufficiency, with sufficiency tested by \emph{composition}
--- is the transferable method here.

\textbf{Relational computation as declension.} The measurements invite a
morphological reading, offered as an organizing metaphor rather than a
claim: the same stem takes different endings, the same ending applies
across stems (\S{}4.5), two cases share a surface form without being the
same case (\textbf{syncretism}, \S{}4.4), stem and ending compose with a
small, behaviorally inert residue of \textbf{fusion} (\S{}4.3), and the
composed stem-plus-ending is a usable form (\S{}4.2). The limits: unlike
morphological case, the marking is continuous, graded, and
\textbf{layer-dependent} --- a trajectory, not a static affix (\S{}4.6) ---
and arithmetic and logic do not decline (\S{}4.8). The honest formulation
is that entities acquire \textbf{transient relational configurations
partially comparable to grammatical case}, not that the model implements
a case grammar.

\section{Conclusion}

An LLM represents a relational operation at three verifiable levels: the
operator \textbf{exists} as a stable direction --- identically the
factorization\textquotesingle s operator component; it \textbf{causally
controls} the answer margin --- every swap flips, localized at the query
token, vanishing under permuted labels; and the factorization is
\textbf{behaviorally sufficient} --- a trajectory composed from its
additive parts, no interaction term, makes the model produce the answer
at its own accuracy level, by replacement or by calibrated addition. It
generalizes to unseen operands, rewordings, a second domain, and a
second architecture; it does not emerge for arithmetic or logic under
our setup. The gap between shifting a preference and executing an
operation, where it appeared, was a property of the intervention, not
the representation.

\section{Limitations}

\textbf{Scale and family:} 1.7--9B models from two decoder-only
families; no \ensuremath{\geq}30B model; relational linearity holds for a subset of
relations even in-domain (cf. LRE \textasciitilde48\%).
\textbf{Sufficiency is scoped:} "generates" means short-decode
containment (3 tokens) against the model\textquotesingle s own clean
accuracy as ceiling, on short single-query prompts; composition and dose
\ensuremath{\times} position replicate at 8B (\S{}4.2); the leave-one-out composition reaches
36\%, not the full-grid 52\% --- some of the composed
state\textquotesingle s precision does come from seeing the cell.
\textbf{Coverage:} five country + four animal relations, English-only;
three context frames and a full re-lexicalization (\S{}4.5); other entity
types and languages open. \textbf{Arithmetic:} single-digit regime
(BPE), at scales where arithmetic competence is itself limited (\S{}4.8).
\textbf{Reading-position result} restates known subject-enrichment \ensuremath{\to}
attribute-extraction dynamics. \textbf{No J-space claim} (\S{}4.9, Appendix
B).
