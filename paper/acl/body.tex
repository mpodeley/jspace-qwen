\section{Introduction}

When a language model completes \emph{"The currency of Italy is---"},
does it represent \emph{currency-of-Italy} as one fused thing, or as an
entity plus an operation applied to it? This paper argues, causally, for
the second reading --- and asks the question a claim like that must
answer at \textbf{three separate levels of evidence}:
\textbf{representation} (does a stable relational direction
\emph{exist}, separable from the operand?), \textbf{causal influence}
(does intervening on it systematically move the model\textquotesingle s
\emph{relative preference} between answers?), and \textbf{behavioral
sufficiency} (can the factorized parts make the model actually
\emph{produce} the target answer?). Distinguishing the levels matters
because interventions that move a logit margin are routinely read as if
they controlled behavior, and the gap between the two is where
interpretability claims quietly overreach. Here that gap becomes the
object of study --- and resolves: we show it is a property of \emph{how}
the direction is applied (mode and dose), not of \emph{what} it
contains.

A line of work shows that a \emph{task} or \emph{relation} can be
captured by a single addable vector in an LLM\textquotesingle s residual
stream (task vectors, \citealp{hendel2023icl}; function vectors, \citealp{todd2024function}) and that many relations are approximately linear operators (LRE,
\citealp{hernandez2024lre}; \citealp{merullo2024vector}). What has not been
characterized is the \textbf{joint structure of operator and operand}:
whether the operation \emph{factorizes} from its argument, whether that
factorization \emph{generalizes}, whether the operation is separable
from the \emph{word it produces}, whether the factorized parts suffice
to \emph{drive generation}, and whether any of this holds \emph{beyond}
relational facts. We answer these on Qwen3 and Gemma-2, across a
geographic and an animal-taxonomy domain, with arithmetic and comparison
logic as the contrast where the factorization does not emerge. A closing
discussion (\S{}5) offers a morphological reading --- relational
computation as \textbf{declension} --- as an organizing metaphor for the
asymmetries we observe.

\section{Related work and what is new
here}

\textbf{Extracting relational concepts.} The closest prior work is \citet{wang2024locating}, who locate hidden states that express a relation
separately from its subject, transplant these relational representations
between subjects, and rewrite relations across 22 relation types and
several models. We instead characterize the \textbf{joint geometry} of
relation and entity: we quantify its additive and interaction components
(the two-way ANOVA with a measured fusion term), run exhaustive
all-pairs swaps against matched-norm nulls, and test whether relation
directions generalize causally across held-out entities, prompt
contexts, architectures, and task domains --- including the domains
where the factorization fails.

\textbf{Operation as an addable vector; relation as a linear operator.}
Task Vectors (\citealp{hendel2023icl}) and Function Vectors (Todd et
al., ICLR 2024) extract one vector per ICL task and add/patch it to
trigger the task (similarly In-Context Vectors, \citealp{liu2024icv};
ActAdd, \citealp{turner2023actadd}) --- but bundle operator \emph{and} operand
into one unfactored "task" direction, without competitive nulls. LRE
(\citealp{hernandez2024lre}) fits a full affine map per relation --- a
\emph{monolithic} operator. Summing Up the Facts (\citealp{chughtai2024summing})
decomposes factual recall into additive \emph{circuits}; ours is a
two-way ANOVA of the \textbf{residual state itself}, with a measured
interaction term and a sufficiency test on the components.

\textbf{Attribute subspaces / reading position.} RAVEL (\citealp{huang2024ravel}) disentangles multiple \emph{attributes of one entity} into
subspaces (operators sharing an operand); we measure the orthogonal
axis, \textbf{operator vs operand}. The operand\ensuremath{\to}operator shift along the
sequence restates the subject-enrichment \ensuremath{\to} attribute-extraction dynamics
of \citet{geva2023dissecting} in operator/operand terms --- our least
novel point, included as confirmation.

\textbf{Global workspace and the Jacobian lens.} \citet{gurnee2026workspace} introduce the Jacobian lens and identify a
mid-depth "global workspace" band. We adopt their depth band as the site
of intervention; our contribution is orthogonal --- the operator/operand
structure of what the band \emph{holds} --- and we report a controlled
null on the lens\textquotesingle s readout advantage for our task
(\S{}4.9).

\textbf{Arithmetic and logic.} LLM arithmetic is reported as a "bag of
heuristics" (\citealp{nikankin2025heuristics}) and Fourier/helical procedures (Nanda
et al. 2023; \citealp{zhong2023clock}; \citealp{kantamneni2025trig}), with the
operand \emph{value} cleanly linear (\citealp{gurnee2024space}); truth is
steerable (\citealp{marks2023geometry}) and logical-operator heads exist (Hong
et al. 2024). No prior work reports a \textbf{cross-domain
operator/operand factorization}; we provide one and show where it does
not emerge.

\textbf{What is new here is the combination, not any single ingredient.}
That relations admit addable vectors (task/function vectors) and linear
decoders (LRE) is established; what has not been done is the joint,
quantified analysis: (1) an operator \ensuremath{\oplus} operand \textbf{factorization of
the residual state itself} (two-way ANOVA with a measured
interaction/fusion term), with the steering vector shown to \emph{be}
the ANOVA operator component; (2) an \textbf{exhaustive all-pairs causal
swap} validated against a competitive null battery --- matched-norm
random, random \emph{within the operator subspace}, and directions
rebuilt under \textbf{permuted relation labels}; (3)
\textbf{held-out-operand generalization} as the test separating a
transferable direction from interpolation among build examples; (4) a
\textbf{compositional sufficiency} test: patching a state assembled from
the factorization\textquotesingle s parts and asking whether the model
\emph{generates} the target answer --- including a dose \ensuremath{\times} position
calibration showing where additive steering itself generates; (5) the
\textbf{operation-vs-realization dissociation} (language \ensuremath{\neq} demonym as
directions despite identical output); and (6) a \textbf{cross-domain
test} (a second retrieval domain where the factorization replicates;
arithmetic and logic where it does not emerge). The declension/case
reading is our \emph{presentation} of these measurements --- an
organizing metaphor that anticipates the observed asymmetries
(syncretism at the exponent, fusion in the interaction term) --- not an
additional empirical claim.

\section{Method}

\textbf{Setup.} Qwen3-1.7B/8B throughout, plus Gemma-2-9B for the
cross-architecture replication (\S{}4.7); all run in bf16 on a single AMD
Strix Halo APU (environment details in the supplementary repository). A
relation is rendered into a template; the canonical frame is
\texttt{"The\ \{op\}\ of\ \{a\}\ is"}, and \S{}4.5 additionally uses two
paraphrase frames (question--answer and discourse-prefixed) that hold
the \texttt{\{op\}\ of\ \{a\}} unit fixed while varying the surrounding
frame, all ending in \emph{is} so answer scoring is comparable. Reading
position is the query token unless stated; the depth "workspace" band
(38--92\% of layers) is where \citet{gurnee2026workspace} locates the global
workspace and where we build operator directions.

\textbf{Notation.} The model\textquotesingle s state is a grid
\texttt{h\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},t\}\ \ensuremath{\in}\ R\^{}d}: one residual-stream vector per layer
\texttt{\ensuremath{\ell}} per token position \texttt{t}; every measurement fixes both
indices. Two reading positions recur: the \textbf{entity token} (the
operand\textquotesingle s last subword, \texttt{t\ =\ -2} in the
canonical frame) and the \textbf{query token} (the final prompt token,
\texttt{t\ =\ -1}, where the next-token prediction is read; "query"
means reading position, not attention\textquotesingle s Q). Rendering
the operator--operand grid through one template gives, at each
\texttt{(\ensuremath{\ell},\ t)}, a family of states \texttt{h\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},t\}(o,\ k)}.

\textbf{Operator direction \ensuremath{\equiv} factorization component.} For operator
\texttt{k},
\texttt{v\_\ensuremath{\ell}(k)\ =\ mean\_o{[}\ h\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},-1\}(o,\ k)\ -\ mean\_\{k\textquotesingle{}\}\ h\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},-1\}(o,\ k\textquotesingle{})\ {]}}
--- one direction \textbf{per layer} of the workspace band (13 layers
per model), the function-vector construction. This is
\emph{algebraically identical} to the operator main effect of the
two-way factorization below
(\texttt{b\_\ensuremath{\ell}(k)\ =\ mean\_o\ h\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},-1\}(\textperiodcentered{},k)\ -\ \ensuremath{\mu}\_\ensuremath{\ell}}), and we verify
the identity numerically (max relative deviation 8.5\ensuremath{\times}\ensuremath{10^{-8}} across the
band). Everything the paper does --- steering, composing, decoding ---
manipulates this one object; the differences between experiments are
differences in \emph{how it is applied}, never in what is extracted.

\textbf{Factorization.} At a fixed layer and position,
\texttt{h\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},t\}(o,\ k)\ =\ \ensuremath{\mu}\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},t\}\ +\ a\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},t\}(o)\ +\ b\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},t\}(k)\ +\ e\_\{\ensuremath{\ell},t\}(o,\ k)}
--- operand main effect, operator main effect, and interaction, exact by
construction (the interaction is the cell residual); we report the
variance share of each term (Appendix A). Table 1 uses the mid-workspace
layer --- \textbf{layer 17 of 28 (63\% depth) at 1.7B, 23 of 36 at 8B,
27 of 42 on Gemma-2-9B} --- at \texttt{t\ =\ -1} and, as the control,
\texttt{t\ =\ -2}.

\textbf{Swap, positions, and controls.} Efficacy of
\texttt{k\_A\ \ensuremath{\to}\ k\_B} is the change in
\texttt{logit(answer\_B)\ -\ logit(answer\_A)} at the query position
when \texttt{\ensuremath{\alpha}\textperiodcentered{}(v(k\_B)\ -\ v(k\_A))} is added --- over the band or a
single layer, at all prompt positions or a chosen one (all / query /
operand / sentence-initial control). Beyond the \textbf{matched-norm
random} direction, the null battery includes directions rebuilt under
\textbf{permuted relation labels} (per-operand and global --- same
statistics, no semantics: the decisive null), random directions
\textbf{inside the operator subspace}, the correct direction at the
\textbf{wrong layer} and \textbf{wrong position}, an
\textbf{other-relation} direction of matched norm, and
\textbf{shuffled-answer} scoring. Every intervention reports margin
change, normalized margin, target rank before/after, top-1, greedy exact
match (3 tokens), on-task KL, and off-task KL on unrelated WikiText ---
not flips alone. Answers are scored at their distinguishing first token
(bare digit for arithmetic); a tokenization guard drops, per operator
pair, operands whose answers collide there (4 of 12 survive on the
syncretic demonym\ensuremath{\leftrightarrow}language pairs; all 12 elsewhere).

\textbf{Composition (behavioral sufficiency).} The
factorization\textquotesingle s component vectors, computed per layer,
are recombined and \textbf{replace} the residual at the query position
(\texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}+operator}, \texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}+operand}, \texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}+operand+operator},
\texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}+interaction}, the full donor, a norm-matched magnitude
control) --- plus two loophole-closers: a \textbf{leave-one-cell-out}
reconstruction whose components never saw the target cell, and a
\textbf{wrong-operand} composition that should (and does) redirect the
answer. Greedy generation over 3 tokens is the readout, against the
model\textquotesingle s own clean accuracy as ceiling.

\textbf{Generalization and domains.} Build \texttt{v(op)} from half the
operands; test the swap on the held-out half --- a genuine operator vs.
interpolation among build examples. Domains: five country relations
(currency, capital, language, demonym, continent \ensuremath{\times} 12 countries), an
independently curated \textbf{animal-taxonomy domain} (class, habitat,
diet, covering \ensuremath{\times} 12 animals; stable 1-to-1 answers, tokenizer-screened,
three paraphrase frames), and arithmetic / comparison logic as the
contrast domains.

\textbf{Statistical treatment.} The ordered swaps are \textbf{not}
independent observations: they share operator directions and operands.
We report cluster-bootstrap percentile intervals (10,000 replicates) at
two levels: within a pair the \textbf{operand} is the resampling unit;
across the paradigm the \textbf{operator} is the top-level cluster (a
dyadic node bootstrap --- resample operators with replacement, weight
each pair by its endpoints\textquotesingle{} multiplicities, resample
operands within surviving pairs). Per-operand values are released as
long-form artifacts (Appendix A).

\textbf{J-space controls.} We repeat readout-geometry in the J-lens
readout \texttt{unembed(J\textperiodcentered{}h)} vs the logit-lens readout
\texttt{unembed(h)}, and re-run efficacy with \textbf{spectrum-matched
random-projection} and \textbf{permuted-vocabulary} null lenses
(Appendix B).

\subsection{Relational operators are causally manipulable --- and
the effect is localized and
label-specific}

All 20 ordered operator swaps flip the \textbf{target-vs-source logit
margin} (the sign of \texttt{logit(answer\_B)\ -\ logit(answer\_A)} at
the distinguishing token) while the matched-norm random control is \ensuremath{\approx} 0.
Under the operator-level cluster bootstrap (\S{}3, the level that respects
that pairs share directions), the swap-random contrast is \textbf{+22.6,
95\% CI {[}+14.0, +32.1{]}} at 1.7B, \textbf{+26.0 {[}+17.9, +32.8{]}}
at 8B, and \textbf{+30.4 {[}+23.8, +34.8{]}} on Gemma-2-9B; the flip
fraction is \textbf{1.00 {[}1.00, 1.00{]}} in all three --- every
replicate flips every pair. Per-pair operand-bootstrap CIs never cross
zero, and the weakest pairs are exactly the syncretic ones (demonym \ensuremath{\leftrightarrow}
language, which share their surface form; full per-pair distributions in
Appendix A).

\textbf{Where the vector has to land (1.7B, \ensuremath{\alpha} = 4).} Injecting at the
\textbf{query token alone} reproduces the all-position effect on the
margin --- at 60\ensuremath{\times} lower off-task cost:

The margin effect is a \textbf{localized edit of the queried relation}
--- position-specific (the operand token and a structurally irrelevant
token do essentially nothing) and nearly free off-task when restricted
to the query position --- not a global perturbation of processing.
(Ranks are medians; n per condition is 224 (pair, operand) cells = 20
pairs \ensuremath{\times} up to 12 operands; the bootstrap unit is stated in \S{}3.)

\textbf{The label is everything.} Directions rebuilt from the
\emph{same} residuals with \textbf{permuted relation labels} ---
preserving every statistical property of the extraction while destroying
its semantics --- abolish the effect: \textbf{+0.7 {[}-0.8, +3.9{]}}
per-operand, +0.6 global (20 redraws each). A random direction
\textbf{inside the operator subspace} (right subspace, wrong content)
also does nothing: +0.75 {[}-2.2, +3.5{]}. Three further probes stay
nonzero for \emph{mechanistically informative} reasons, and decompose
what the margin measures: an \textbf{other-relation} direction retains
the shared \texttt{-v(source)} component and yields \ensuremath{\approx} half the effect
(+11.1 --- uninstalling the source is half the margin);
\textbf{shuffled-answer scoring} still moves (+21.1) because
\texttt{+v(target)} boosts the whole answer \emph{category} (any capital
city, not specifically Rome) --- operand specificity lives in
generation, which \S{}4.2 measures directly; and the correct direction at
the \textbf{wrong (early) layer} retains +9.8 because residual-stream
additions \emph{persist} downstream --- per-layer locality is measured
properly in \S{}4.6. (Forest plot in Appendix A.)

\textbf{Dose--response, both signs.} The effect is a \textbf{signed
causal axis}: the operator-specific margin shift is monotone in \ensuremath{\alpha}
through zero and \emph{reverses} when the direction is inverted ---
+22.6 at \ensuremath{\alpha} = +4 versus \textbf{-21.0 at \ensuremath{\alpha} = -4} (an almost exact
mirror), saturating at both ends --- while the random
control\textquotesingle s nonspecific shift (\ensuremath{\approx} +6) is flat in
\textbar \ensuremath{\alpha}\textbar, which is why every headline number is a swap -
random contrast. The positive branch saturates higher (+29 vs. -15 raw
shift) for an interpretable reason: the clean margin already favors the
source relation, so pushing further toward it hits a floor. Off-task
cost grows with dose and with breadth (7.9 \ensuremath{\to} 21 nats/token on unrelated
WikiText across the positive sweep for the band-wide, all-position hook
--- versus 0.29 at the query token, above). What dose does to
\emph{generation} is the subject of \S{}4.2. (Dose curves in Appendix A.)

\subsection{A state composed from the
factorization\textquotesingle s parts generates the
answer}

At the default dose, additive steering flips every \emph{margin} but
rarely makes the model \emph{say} the target answer (0.4--2.2\% exact
match), whereas replacing the query-position residual with a
\textbf{real donor activation} (same operand, target relation) does ---
51\%, at the model\textquotesingle s clean accuracy of 53\%. Is the
donor carrying something the direction lacks --- operand binding, a
non-additive interaction, lexical or execution signals? We decompose the
donor by the exact factorization and patch partial reconstructions at
the query position (every variant includes \ensuremath{\mu}: the hook \emph{replaces}
the state, so a variant must be a plausible full-magnitude residual).

\textbf{The donor carries nothing the additive parts
don\textquotesingle t.} The purely additive composition
\texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}\ +\ operand\ +\ operator} generates at the
donor\textquotesingle s own level (51.8\% vs. 50.9\%) --- the
model\textquotesingle s competence ceiling --- while the interaction
term alone does nothing (7.1\%, vs. 4.5\% for the magnitude control).
Two controls close the loopholes. A \textbf{leave-one-cell-out}
reconstruction, whose operand and operator components were built
\emph{without ever seeing the target cell}, still generates at 35.7\%
--- composition, not leakage. And swapping in a \textbf{wrong
operand\textquotesingle s} component redirects the answer to \emph{that}
operand (34.4\% says-other vs. 1.8\% baseline): the state is
compositional in exactly the way the factorization claims --- the
operator component selects the relation, the operand component selects
whose answer. At \textbf{8B every number sharpens}: composed 62.1\% vs.
donor 65.2\% (clean ceiling 68\%), leave-one-out \textbf{50.4\%},
wrong-operand redirect \textbf{48.2\%}, interaction-only 3.6\%,
magnitude control 2.2\%.

\textbf{Sufficiency by steering, at the calibrated dose.} Since
\texttt{v(op)} \emph{is} the operator component (\S{}3), adding
\texttt{\ensuremath{\alpha}\textperiodcentered{}(v(B)\ -\ v(A))} at the query position of one layer lands, at
\ensuremath{\alpha} = 1, on \texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}\ +\ operand\ +\ operator(B)\ +\ interaction(A)} ---
the composed state up to a wrong interaction term. The dose sweep
confirms it behaviorally: generation follows an \textbf{inverted-U
peaked exactly at the on-manifold dose} --- 38.4\% exact match at \ensuremath{\alpha} = 1
for a single layer, \textbf{51.3\% at \ensuremath{\alpha} \ensuremath{\approx} 0.1 for the 13-layer band}
(band additions accumulate, so the band\textquotesingle s on-manifold
dose is \ensuremath{\approx} 1/13 per layer) --- while past the peak the margin keeps
climbing (+28.9 at \ensuremath{\alpha} = 4) as generation collapses (0.4\%) and the
on-task distribution degrades (KL 29 nats). The earlier "steering moves
margins but cannot generate" result was an \textbf{overdose artifact},
not an information deficit. The factorized representation is thus
\textbf{compositionally sufficient} --- by replacement and by calibrated
addition --- and the gap between moving a preference and producing an
answer is a property of \emph{how} the direction is applied, not of
\emph{what} it contains. (At a single layer, \texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}\ +\ operator}
alone already generates at 44.6\% \ensuremath{\approx} the donor\textquotesingle s 40.6\%
there: unpatched layers and positions still carry the operand --- the
operator injection point is local, operand identity distributed;
consistently, the wrong-operand redirect disappears there, 1.3\%.)

\subsection{Operation \ensuremath{\neq} realization
(syncretism)}

\emph{language-of} and \emph{demonym-of} emit the \textbf{same word}
(Italian) yet are \textbf{distinct operator directions}; the swap
between them is weak precisely because they share their output word. An
\textbf{exponent-free operator marker} built from the shared-output
pairs (\texttt{mean{[}h(language)\ -\ h(demonym){]}}, the output word
cancelled by construction) still installs the relation causally (1.7B:
-3.6 \ensuremath{\to} +12.5): the operator representation is not an output-word
representation (\S{}5 reads this as syncretism).

(Operator-direction cosines and the exponent-free-marker bar chart are
in Appendix A.)

\subsection{Operators generalize: held-out operands, prompt frames,
wordings --- and a second
domain}

\textbf{Held-out operands.} \texttt{v(op)} built on 6 operands and
applied to the 6 held-out operands still flips \textbf{20/20} swaps ---
a genuine, transferable operator, not interpolation. Operator-level
cluster bootstrap on the held-out contrast: \textbf{+20.0 {[}+10.8,
+29.5{]}} at 1.7B, \textbf{+22.8 {[}+14.0, +31.0{]}} at 8B; flip
fraction \textbf{1.00 {[}1.00, 1.00{]}} at both.

\textbf{Frames and wordings.} To rule out template echo we render every
relation in three paraphrase frames (declarative, question--answer,
discourse-prefixed) and test every build\ensuremath{\to}test combination: on 1.7B
\textbf{all 9 combinations flip 20/20} (180/180), with the contrast
\emph{frame-invariant to two decimals} (+22.6 on all three test frames
from declarative-built directions); at 8B 100/100 with the same
invariance (+26.0 / +26.0 / +26.0). The stronger question is whether the
direction survives changing the \emph{wording of the relation itself}:
we re-lexicalize every relation (\emph{currency of} \ensuremath{\to} \emph{money used
in}, \emph{capital of} \ensuremath{\to} \emph{seat of government in}, \ldots) and test
both transfer directions --- \textbf{40/40 at 1.7B and 40/40 at 8B},
with transfer contrasts indistinguishable from within-formulation ones
(+22.59 transferred vs. +22.62 within at 1.7B). Clean baselines shift
across frames and wordings --- the prompts are genuinely different ---
but the operator\textquotesingle s causal effect does not: it is a
property of the \textbf{relation}, not of its wording.

\textbf{A second, non-geographic domain.} Every signature replicates on
an independently curated animal-taxonomy domain (class / habitat / diet
/ covering \ensuremath{\times} 12 animals; 1.7B): \textbf{12/12} ordered swaps flip (+18.1
{[}+13.5, +22.8{]}); held-out operands \textbf{12/12} at +18.0 ---
transfer indistinguishable from within; cross-frame \textbf{72/72},
again frame-invariant to two decimals; permuted-label and subspace nulls
at zero (-0.5 and +0.2, CIs spanning 0); the same reading-position
signature (operand 79\% at the entity token, operator 62\% at the query
token); and even the same quantitative margin decomposition (the
other-relation probe retains \ensuremath{\approx} half the effect in both domains: 47\% vs.
49\%). Two honest caveats: the animal factorization carries more
interaction (22\% vs. 9\% --- informative in \S{}4.8), and the
generation-level readout is floor-limited here (the
model\textquotesingle s own clean greedy accuracy on these multi-token
answers is 6\%), so in this domain sufficiency is attested by margins
and rank, not exact match.

\subsection{Where the operator is represented is not where it
acts}

A per-layer sweep (1.7B; direction built \textbf{and} injected at each
single layer) separates three notions the band framing conflates.
\emph{Trivial readability:} a held-out-operand classifier decodes the
operator at \textbf{100\% from every layer, including layer 0} --- the
operator\textquotesingle s token is in the prompt, so decodability
locates nothing. \emph{Representation:} the ANOVA operator share peaks
\textbf{early} (92\% at 19\% depth), decaying to 57\%. \emph{Causal
leverage:} the single-layer swap contrast is \ensuremath{\approx} 0 early and peaks
\textbf{late} (+21.3 {[}+13.8, +28.2{]} at 89\% depth --- one layer
there reproduces the full band\textquotesingle s +22.6). The two
informative curves peak \textbf{70 depth-points apart}: where the
operator is most cleanly factorized is not where injecting it most moves
the output. This explains the wrong-layer probe of \S{}4.1 (early additions
act by persisting into later layers) and diagnoses the mid-band layer of
\S{}4.1--4.2 as far from optimal (L17: +6.8 vs. L24: +21.3): being
\emph{readable}, being \emph{the representation}, and being
\emph{causally potent} are three different layer profiles of one
direction. (Figure in Appendix A.)

\subsection{Cross-architecture replication
(Gemma-2-9B)}

The entire pipeline transfers unchanged to \textbf{Gemma-2-9B} --- a
different pretraining corpus, tokenizer (SentencePiece; answer
single-token coverage 0.95), and architecture family (soft-capped
logits, GQA). Every qualitative signature replicates, with the strongest
effects of the three models: all-pairs contrast \textbf{+30.4 {[}+23.8,
+34.8{]}} (20/20 flips), held-out \textbf{+26.6 {[}+20.6, +33.2{]}},
operator variance 84.8\% with \textbf{6.8\%} interaction, and the
exponent-free marker -3.0 \ensuremath{\to} +8.5 (full three-model table in Appendix A).
Raw logit units are not directly comparable across architectures
(Gemma-2 soft-caps its final logits), so cross-model comparison should
lean on flip rates and variance shares, which match or exceed
Qwen3\textquotesingle s: the factorization is not an idiosyncrasy of one
model family.

much fusion the state carries}. This is a \textbf{domain contrast,
strictly scoped}: the factorization observed in relational retrieval
does not emerge for arithmetic \textbf{under our models, tokenization
choices, prompts, and intervention procedure} --- a regime where 1.7B/8B

the grid, and the operation may be computed distributively or
iteratively rather than retrieved. We claim the contrast, not a theorem
about algorithmic computation.

\textbf{Reconciling a contrary report.} \citet{christ2025structure} find that
\textbf{add-N} operators (a fixed addend \texttt{N} over one number)
\emph{do} generalize. The cuts differ: add-N varies a linear numeric
\emph{parameter} --- effectively an operand on the number line (Gurnee
\& Tegmark 2024) --- while our \texttt{+\ \ensuremath{\times}\ -} cut varies the
\emph{function}. Running both cuts on Qwen3 places add-N between
relations and \texttt{+\ \ensuremath{\times}\ -} (held-out 5/12 \textperiodcentered{} 6/12), and its
directions are by far the most collinear of the three families (76\% of
the operator-set variance on one line --- a number line, not a set of
operations). Their positive and our negative coexist without
contradiction; the full table is in Appendix A.

\subsection{This is causal structure, not a J-space readout
advantage}

This project began as a replication of the J-space readout claim of
\citealp{gurnee2026workspace}, whose "workspace" band we
intervene on throughout. Under matched controls --- including a
spectrum-matched random projection --- that readout showed \textbf{no
advantage} over the logit lens on any of four comparisons (Appendix B):
the structure here is causal organization, not a readable subspace.

\section{Discussion}

\textbf{Modes of intervention, and what a margin measures.} What looked
like a gap between influencing a preference and executing an operation
was a gap between \emph{ways of applying the same vector}: a large added
multiple moves margins monotonically while pushing the state
off-manifold (generation dies); a calibrated multiple, or replacement
with the composed \texttt{\ensuremath{\mu}\ +\ operand\ +\ operator}, generates at the
model\textquotesingle s ceiling (\S{}4.2). The null battery likewise
decomposes the margin itself --- half uninstalling the source relation,
half installing the target \emph{category}, with operand specificity
visible only in generation (\S{}4.1). Both points travel: steering studies
that report margin shifts at one fixed dose are measuring category-level
preference under a possibly off-manifold intervention, and their
negative generation results do not, by themselves, show the information
is absent. The three-level discipline --- representation, influence,
sufficiency, with sufficiency tested by \emph{composition} --- is the
transferable method here.

\textbf{Relational computation as declension.} The measurements invite a
morphological reading, offered as an organizing metaphor rather than a
claim. In a declining language a noun\textquotesingle s role is marked
by a case ending on a stem: the same stem takes different endings (one
country, five directions), the same ending applies across stems
(held-out transfer, \S{}4.5), two cases can share a surface form without
being the same case (\textbf{syncretism} --- \emph{language-of} and
\emph{demonym-of} both emit "Italian" yet are distinct directions, \S{}4.4:
a case marker stripped of its exponent remains causally functional), and
stem and ending compose with a small residue of \textbf{fusion} (the
\textasciitilde10\% interaction, \S{}4.3 --- behaviorally inert, \S{}4.2). The
metaphor marks its own limits: arithmetic and logic operators do not
decline (\S{}4.8).

\section{Conclusion}

An LLM represents a relational operation at three verifiable levels. The
operator \textbf{exists} as a stable direction --- identically the
factorization\textquotesingle s operator component. It \textbf{causally
controls} the answer margin: every ordered swap flips, localized at the
query token, vanishing under permuted labels. And the factorization is
\textbf{behaviorally sufficient}: a state composed from its additive
parts --- no interaction term --- makes the model produce the target
answer at its own competence ceiling, by replacement or by calibrated
addition. The structure generalizes to unseen operands, rewordings, a
second retrieval domain, and a second architecture; it separates the
operation from the word that realizes it; it does not emerge for
arithmetic or logic under our setup. The gap between shifting a
preference and executing an operation, where it appeared, was a property
of the intervention, not of the representation.

\section{Limitations}

\textbf{Scale and family:} 1.7--9B models from two decoder-only
families; no \ensuremath{\geq}30B model; relational linearity holds for a subset of
relations even in-domain (cf. LRE \textasciitilde48\%).
\textbf{Sufficiency is scoped:} "generates" means greedy exact match
over three tokens against the model\textquotesingle s own clean accuracy
as ceiling, on short single-query prompts; composition and dose \ensuremath{\times}
position are at 1.7B (8B replication in the artifacts); the
leave-one-out composition reaches 36\%, not the full-grid 52\% --- some
of the composed state\textquotesingle s precision does come from seeing
the cell. \textbf{Coverage:} five country + four animal relations,
English-only; three context frames and a full re-lexicalization (\S{}4.5);
other entity types and languages open. \textbf{Arithmetic:} single-digit
regime (BPE), at scales where arithmetic competence is itself limited
(\S{}4.8). \textbf{Reading-position result} restates known
subject-enrichment \ensuremath{\to} attribute-extraction dynamics. \textbf{No J-space
claim} (\S{}4.9, Appendix B).
